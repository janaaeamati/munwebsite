What is the topic and what do I have to know?

The topic that we will be debating this year at TriMUN is Drug Trafficking.
Drug trafficking is a pervasive issue with far-reaching consequences that extend beyond national borders, making it highly relevant on a worldwide scale. The illicit trade of drugs fuels transnational crime networks, fostering a complex web of criminal activities, corruption, and violence. The global nature of drug trafficking intertwines economies, politics, and security, impacting both developed and developing nations. Its implications are evident in the destabilization of governments, the exacerbation of social disparities, and the undermining of public health efforts. Furthermore, drug trafficking contributes to the emergence of global health challenges, as substances cross borders, affecting diverse communities and straining international efforts to address addiction and related health issues. Given these multifaceted repercussions, the fight against drug trafficking necessitates international cooperation, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nations in combating this pervasive threat.

A number of individuals from each team will be assigned different countries. Normally, for an MUN conference, delegates (the attendees) have to do their own research and write a position paper. To make this experience as beginner friendly as possible we have built in time so that the groups of delegates can research their countries' positions and topic on the day of the training MUN to guide them through this process.
